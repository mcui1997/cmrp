转换 The Conversion Machine

Paying a small tax now to erase a big one later

A Roth conversion moves money out of the Traditional account, where every future dollar is taxed, and into the Roth, where nothing ever is. The price is ordinary income tax today on the amount moved. The whole game is choosing years when that price is low.

Why the golden window is golden

Converting $100,000 costs about while the salary is still there, but only about in a retirement year with no salary. Same money, same destination. The only difference is the year you choose.

While working, a conversion sits on top of the salary and is taxed at the top. In the golden window it starts at the bottom of the brackets, where the first dollars are taxed at 10 and 12 percent, and the standard deduction makes the first slice free.

    The rules the plan follows
    • The 24 percent bracket is a ceiling, never a target. Recalculate before every conversion.
    • Conversions happen in stages, in November and December, after a fresh tax projection.
    • Conversion tax is paid from cash, never from the retirement accounts themselves.
    • Conversions stop by 70. By then the Traditional account should be nearly empty.
    • One quiet reason to convert aggressively: a surviving spouse files single, where the same income lands in higher brackets. Money already in the Roth never faces that.

    取钱 Where Money Comes From

    Every year, spending has an order

    This is the part most plans skip. It is not just how much you spend, it is which account each dollar comes from. The order below protects the growth accounts and keeps taxes low.

    1. Cash above the reserve. Already taxed, always available. Through 64 the plan spends cash only.
    2. Brokerage. Lightly taxed. Used before touching retirement accounts.
    3. Traditional. Taxed as ordinary income, so it comes late, and conversions should leave little here anyway.
    4. Roth. Used last, and always tax free. In this plan it quietly carries the years just before Social Security, then rests and compounds as the legacy.

    The cash bridge and the reserve, explained

    From 60 to 64 there is no salary and no Social Security, so cash carries everything. The house sale at 60, about $800,000, plus the $200,000 saved during the working years, is that bridge. From 65 on, the plan protects a reserve of 18 months of spending so a bad market never forces a sale. From 70, Social Security covers most of the budget and the portfolio mostly rests.

    中国 The China Layer

    What actually changes when you go home

    Less than you might fear. As a US citizen your taxes, your accounts, and this whole plan work the same from Shenzhen as from Dallas. Four things need real attention.

    1 · Healthcare, chapter by chapter

    Until 60. The employer plan, as today.

    60 to 64, in China. Chinese healthcare paid directly. Excellent care at a fraction of US prices, which is exactly why the plan holds medical surprises of $10,000 to $45,000 instead of US sized ones. Optional travel cover for US visits.

    65 and on. US Medicare begins August 2035, and it pays nothing outside the US. Enroll in Part A, which is free. Part B is a real decision, read below.

    The Part B decision

    Part B costs a premium every month and only helps if you are treated in the US. Skipping it saves money but adds a lifetime late penalty of about 10 percent per year skipped if you ever return and want it. Take it if a move back is realistic, skip it if China is truly permanent. Decide, do not drift.

    2 · Keep the US machinery running

    • A permanent US address, likely your son's, on every account.
    • A US phone number that can receive verification codes.
    • Brokerages can restrict accounts that show a foreign home address. Keep the US address current and do not volunteer the move.
    • Texas as the final state of residence. No state income tax follows you.
    • US tax returns every year, forever, as a citizen. Nothing about the move changes the Roth math.

    3 · Money across the border

    • Social Security is paid to US citizens living in China. Keep the SSA address and bank details current.
    • Move spending money with ordinary bank transfers, in sensible amounts, and keep records.
    • Dad is a Chinese citizen and his costs live inside your budget already. Nothing extra to model, one thing to confirm below.

    4 · Questions for the professionals

    • Advisor: once China treats you as a tax resident, usually after about six years, how will it tax Roth withdrawals and Social Security? China has no concept of a Roth. This is the single sharpest question in the plan.
    • Attorney: the unlimited marital deduction does not apply to a spouse who is not a US citizen. Likely harmless at this estate size, but have it confirmed while drafting the Texas will and trust.
    • CPA: confirm the house basis and the $500,000 exclusion before the sale, and remember it generally requires selling within a few years of moving out.
    • HR: confirm the SERP payout schedule. It cannot be rolled over or converted, it simply pays out and is taxed when it does.

    Guardrails, for the bad years

    If markets fall hard, the plan does not panic and it does not cut essentials. For two years it trims the flexible lines only: travel by 30 percent, hobbies by half, gifts by 10 percent, flexible spending by 40 percent. Essentials are protected first, legacy is optimized last.
